Cenlar Options

  • Forbearance: Temporarily reduces or suspends mortgage payments during financial hardship
  • Loan Modification: Changes loan terms to make payments more affordable, may include:
    • Extending the loan term
    • Reducing interest rates
    • Restructuring payments to fit borrower's budget
    • Moving missed payments to the end of the loan
  • Repayment Plans: Allows gradual catch-up on missed payments over time
  • Reinstatement: Option to pay missed amounts in a lump sum if able
  • Payment Deferral: Moves missed payments to the end of the loan term
  • Special Disaster Assistance: Available for natural disasters

Eligibility Requirements for Cenlar Assistance

  • Financial hardship (job loss, illness, disaster, divorce)
  • Documentation of hardship must be provided
  • Property must be residential (primary residence preferred)
  • Ability to resume payments after temporary assistance
  • For loan modifications, must demonstrate ability to make modified payments

Cenlar Assistance Application Process

  1. Contact Cenlar at 1-800-2CENLAR (1-800-223-6527)
  2. Prepare documentation of hardship and financial situation
  3. Complete required application forms
  4. Work with assigned representative
  5. For loan modifications, may need to complete a trial payment period
  6. Sign and return final agreement documents

Bank of America Credit Card Assistance

Credit Card Hardship Programs

  • Customer Assistance Program: Available to help customers experiencing financial hardship manage credit card debt
  • Payment Relief Options:
    • Lower Interest Rates: Temporary reduction from 20-30% down to as low as 7-8% (or even 0% in some cases)
    • Fee Waivers: Elimination of late fees, overdraft fees, and monthly maintenance fees
    • Payment Deferrals: Option to temporarily postpone payments during hardship periods
    • Reduced Minimum Payments: Lower monthly payment requirements to fit reduced income situations
    • Debt Management Plans: Partnerships with credit counseling agencies for long-term debt solutions

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for Bank of America credit card hardship assistance:

  • Must have a documented financial hardship (job loss, medical emergency, natural disaster, divorce)
  • Situation must be compelling - simply wanting a lower rate is not sufficient
  • May need to demonstrate ability to make reduced payments
  • Must typically stop using the credit card during the program period
  • Credit cards must have been open for a minimum period (varies by program)

How to Apply

  1. Contact Bank of America:
    • Consumer Cards: 855-891-3401
    • Small Business Cards: 888-306-4949
    • International: 1-757-677-4701
  2. Online options:
    • Log in to Online Banking at www.bankofamerica.com
    • Submit request through the Client Assistance Program portal
  3. Be prepared with:
    • Credit card account numbers
    • Details about your financial hardship
    • Current income and expense information
    • Recent financial statements if available
  4. Complete any required documentation promptly
  5. Consider setting up automatic payments to ensure program compliance

Program Details

  • Duration: Hardship programs typically last 3-12 months depending on circumstances
  • Credit Reporting: Participation may be reported to credit bureaus as "paying under a partial payment plan"
  • Credit Limits: May be reduced while in the program
  • Automatic Payments: Often required to ensure timely payments during the program period
  • Interest: May continue to accrue even if payments are reduced or paused

Additional Support Resources

  • Credit Counseling Referrals: Free access to nonprofit credit counseling agencies
  • Financial Education: Resources available at www.bankofamerica.com
  • Better Money Habits: Educational content on budgeting and debt management
  • Debt Management Plans: Longer-term solutions (3-5 years) through partner agencies

American Express Financial Relief Program

Program Features

  • Reduced Interest Rates: Temporary lowering of interest rates on eligible accounts
  • Lower Monthly Payments: Reduced minimum payment requirements
  • Fee Relief: Waiver of late payment fees and other charges
  • Payment Plans: Structured payment arrangements based on financial situation
  • Account Status Protection: Prevention of further delinquency while in the program

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for American Express hardship assistance:

  • Must be experiencing genuine financial hardship
  • May need to demonstrate inability to make regular payments
  • Documentation of hardship may be required
  • Account review will consider delinquency status and prior program enrollment
  • Balance and account history will be evaluated

How to Apply

  1. Contact American Express Financial Relief Program:
    • Phone: 1-866-703-4169
    • Hours: Monday through Friday, 8 a.m. to 10 p.m. ET
  2. Online access:
    • Log in at https://global.americanexpress.com/financial-relief
  3. Prepare to discuss:
    • The nature of your financial hardship
    • Your current income and expenses
    • Proof of financial difficulty if required
    • How long you anticipate needing assistance
  4. Complete the Income & Expenditure form if required
  5. Work with the assigned specialist to determine the best solution

Program Impact and Considerations

  • Card Usage: Your ability to use the card will likely be impacted during the program
  • Credit Score Impact: Enrollment may affect your credit score
  • Credit Limit Changes: Limits may be reduced during the program period
  • Program Duration: Typically designed for temporary relief (varies by situation)
  • Post-Program Options: Additional assistance may be available if hardship continues

Additional Support

  • Debt Management Companies: Amex may refer customers to credit counseling services
  • Financial Education Resources: Available through American Express website
  • Alternative Payment Options: Different arrangements for various hardship situations
  • Specialized Support Teams: Dedicated representatives for hardship assistance

  • Primary Residence: Property must be your primary residence in NY State
  • Risk of Foreclosure: Must be at risk of foreclosure, behind on payments, or struggling with property tax/utility arrears
  • Affordability Post-Assistance: Must demonstrate ability to afford future housing payments after receiving aid
